Manufacturing Process

The production of paperboard follows a systematic manufacturing process designed to ensure consistent quality and efficiency.

  1. Raw Material Collection
  2. Pulp Preparation
  3. Sheet Forming
  4. Pressing
  5. Drying
  6. Surface Coating
  7. Finishing
  8. Converting
  9. Packaging
  10. Distribution

1. Raw Material & Pulp Making

Krishna Tissues uses 100% recycled paper for manufacturing premium paperboard.

The raw materials include:

  • Imported Waste Paper
  • Domestic Waste Paper
  • Old Newspapers (ONP)
  • Mixed Waste Paper
  • White Waste Paper

Advanced pulping equipment removes impurities like sand, wax, pins, plastics, and other unwanted materials.

2. Sheet Forming

The prepared pulp is processed using imported seven-cylinder forming machines.

Different pulp layers are combined to form a strong and durable paperboard sheet suitable for industrial applications.

3. Pressing & Drying

After sheet formation, excess water is removed using advanced pressing systems.

Steam-heated drying cylinders reduce moisture content while improving the strength and surface quality of the board.

4. Coating & Finishing

The board is coated using premium starch, pigments, binders, and additives to achieve:

  • Smooth Surface
  • Better Printing Quality
  • High Gloss Finish
  • Improved Brightness
  • Superior Appearance

5. Converting & Packaging

Finished paperboards are converted into customer-required sizes using modern cutting and winding equipment.

Products are packed carefully using high-quality packaging materials to ensure safe transportation.
